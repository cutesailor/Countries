Capital

The capital of South Korea is Seoul. It is also the country's largest city and serves as its cultural, economic, and political center.

  • Largest city:Seoul is the largest and most populous city in South Korea.
  • Economic and cultural hub: It is a major global city and the center of South Korea's economy, culture, and entertainment.
  • Location: It is situated in the northwest of the country, on the Han River.
  • History: Seoul has a long history, serving as the capital for various Korean dynasties, and contains a mix of historic and modern architecture.

Currency

Korea's currency is the won. Paper money comes in denominations of 1,000; 5,000; 10,000; and 50,000 won bills. Coins come in 1, 5, 10, 100, and 500 won (1 and 5 won coins are not widely circulated).

Official Language

South Korea's official language is Korean, a language often considered a language isolate, and is written using the unique phonetic alphabet called Hangul. Hangul was created in the 15th century to increase literacy and is now the primary script, though Chinese characters (Hanja) are occasionally used for context or older terms. The language is also known for its agglutinative structure, SOV (Subject-Object-Verb) word order, and extensive honorifics that reflect social hierarchy.

Holidays and Traditions

South Korea celebrates major holidays like Seollal (Lunar New Year) and Chuseok (Korean Thanksgiving), which involve family gatherings, ancestral rituals (charye), and traditional foods. Other key holidays include Children's Day (May 5) and Buddha's Birthday (lunar calendar), which have specific traditions like gift-giving for children or lantern parades at temples. Traditions include wearing the hanbok (traditional clothing) and performing deep bows (sebae) to elders.
